Operational Interpretation

At a cost-optimal threshold of 0.30, for every 10,000 transactions this model flags approximately 18 as fraud, of which approximately 15 are genuine fraud and 3 are false alarms. The estimated investigation and friction cost of false alarms is $36, offset against approximately $992 in fraud prevented, for a net saving of approximately $958 compared to no fraud detection.

Cost-Optimal vs F1-Optimal Threshold

For Random Forest, the F1-optimal threshold is 0.69 and the cost-optimal threshold is 0.30. Choosing the F1-optimal threshold instead of the cost-optimal threshold would increase total business cost by approximately $2,165.51 on the validation set.

The F1 metric treats false positives and false negatives symmetrically -- it has no awareness of the dollar asymmetry between a $10 investigation cost and a $500 fraud loss. The cost-optimal threshold internalises that asymmetry directly. A business with reliable estimates of investigation and fraud loss costs should use the cost-optimal threshold. F1-optimal is appropriate when costs are unknown or a balanced trade-off is preferred on principle.

Limitations

  • Anonymized dataset: V1-V28 are PCA-transformed and cannot be interpreted as business features. Transaction amounts may not reflect real currency values.
  • Investigation cost is an estimate: $10 base cost is an industry estimate, not specific to any bank, card scheme, or internal workflow.
  • Concept drift: This dataset is from 2013. Fraud patterns evolve; model performance may degrade on future data without periodic retraining.
  • SMOTE generates synthetic samples: Oversampled minority examples are interpolated, not real transactions. Models may be partially fit to a synthetic distribution rather than real fraud patterns.
  • Random split, not time-ordered: Production models should be trained on past data and evaluated on future data to avoid temporal leakage.
  • Worst-case FN assumption: We assume 100% of the fraud amount is lost. In practice, chargebacks, insurance, or partial recovery reduce actual loss.
